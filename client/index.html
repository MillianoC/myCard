  • About

    Basic Intro

    My name is Chris, and I am from St.Louis. I am currently working on breaking into web development. I hope to someday start my own company, and live somewhere warm. I am getting married in September to Angie, my high school sweetheart. I have a dog and cat, Nova and Gatsby respectively. I love being outside, and have an obsession with good food and beer.



    Early Life

    I grew up in north county, between Hazelwood and Florissant. I graduated from Hazelwood West High School. I have always loved swimming, and consequently spent a lot of my childhood at rivers, lakes, pools, and swim practices. I did a lot of stage work in high school. I also had an early and intense work ethic, working 20-30 hours a week at my retail job, Lids, through my Jr and Sr years.



    After High School

    After a couple years of being a top sales associate at Lids, upon turing 18 on my graduation I was promoted to being an assistant manager. Within a year I was a store manager, and within 2 I was managing one of the top 100 stores in sales. I was being tailored, and on track, to become a district manager before I could legally drink. I grew uncomfortable with some of the company practices, and decided it was not what I wanted to do with my life. I quit, moved home, and re-enrolled at UMSL before my 21st birthday.



    College

    I came back to school not really sure of what I wanted to do, other than be done with retail. I was good at science in high school so I enrolled in some astronomy and math courses. I did well in the classes so I stayed on the course for physics degree. Along the way I developed a strong draw towards psychology and bio-physiology. I became very obsessed with the concept of massive messaging systems, like radiation or hormones, and how they are interpreted into responses, like email or emotions.


    I was a junior when I took my first programming class, and instantly fell in love. I was at a loss. I did not want to essentially start over, yet again, but I knew this is something I could see myself doing for a living.



    LaunchCode

    In the beginning of my senior year I met Paul at LaunchCode, quite on accident. After talking to him for an hour or two I left considering this as a real option. I spent the next couple months spending all of my spare time learning to code at more of an industry level. Coming up to this spring semester I made the decision that I wanted to be a programmer, and that I was willing to take a break from my education to have a chance at it. So I have spent the past few months on a home coding intensive.



    POS Programs

    Mixing my retail/restaurant backgrounds into my programing interests, I have always been oddly obsessed with POS systems. I started with a c/c++ command line version way back when I first talked to LC and my current version being a highly responsive javascript driven web app, with java, php, and swift along the way.



    Other Interests

    As I mentioned I love water and being outside. I tend to spend a lot of weekends camping and in canoes (St.Louis weather permitting). I also work at some high end restaurants, so my fiancee and I have slowly been eating our way through St.Louis. I took my love of beer to the next level this year and started brewing at home. I also enjoy biking, in fact I gave up my car and got by using only my bike and public transit for 2 years.

  • theFly

    This is the big project I am currently working on. I have been working with different options of POS systems for about a year now. It began with a simple hard-coded c++ that stored all of its data in a text file. My POS projects have serverd as a great marker over the course of my evolution as a developer.

    This project is a Single Page App using Node, Angular, HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, and jQuery. At this point only the front-end is availble for public view. The idea behind this project is creating an ecommerce site built up to the point that a smaller company could use this product in place of a tradtional POS and website. That being said, without the back-end you also can not get access to the 'employee' veiw, so what you see is what a customer would see.
